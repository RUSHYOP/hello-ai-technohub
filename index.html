What is AI?

Spoiler: you've been using it all along

No lab coats needed

AI is already in your pocket

Every one of these learned what to do from examples — nobody typed in the rules by hand.

The big difference

AI is not magic. It's maths + data + practice — at a scale no human could ever manage.

How does a machine learn?

The same way you learned to recognise a cat

You, age 3

Nobody gave you cat rules

  • You saw a few cats — someone said “cat!”
  • You guessed wrong sometimes (“dog?”) and got corrected
  • Soon you could spot any cat — fat, tiny, cartoon

A machine, today

Same idea, super-sized

  • Shown millions of labelled photos
  • Guesses, gets corrected, adjusts its internal “dials”
  • Repeats billions of times — this is called training

ChatGPT's secret

A next-word guessing champion

  • Chatbots like ChatGPT, Claude & Gemini read a huge slice of the internet during training
  • They learn which word most likely comes next — and build every answer one word at a time
  • That simple trick, at massive scale, produces essays, code and poetry
  • Careful: it predicts, it doesn't know — so it can be confidently wrong (a “hallucination”)

Agentic AI

The 2025–26 upgrade: from answering… to doing

Chatbot

Answers you

  • You ask → it replies → you do the work
  • “How do I make a study timetable?” → a list of tips
  • Like a very smart friend… who only texts

Agent

Does it for you

  • You give a goal → it plans, acts, checks → done
  • “Make my timetable” → it builds the actual timetable
  • Like an assistant with hands, not just a mouth

Inside an agent

The agent loop

It uses real tools — searching the web, writing code, clicking buttons — and asks you before big decisions.

Not science fiction

What agents already do, in 2026

  • Deep research: read dozens of websites and write a cited report
  • Build software: code, test and fix an entire app from one sentence
  • Use a computer like a human — moving the cursor, clicking, typing
  • Handle errands: compare prices, fill forms, draft bookings — with your OK
  • Work in teams: one agent plans while others build and double-check

Remember this line

Chatbots talk.
Agents get things done.

Agentic AI = giving AI hands, not just a mouth — with a human in charge.

What's next?

Possibilities, careers — and your responsibility

The next 10 years

What might be normal by the time you finish college

  • A personal AI tutor for every student on Earth, in every language
  • Doctors with AI assistants that help spot diseases earlier
  • New medicines & materials discovered in months instead of decades
  • Robots that can see, plan and help at home and in factories
  • Your idea → an app, film or game — without a big team or budget

“With great power…”

Use it with your brain switched on

  • Deepfakes exist: seeing is no longer believing — check the source
  • AI can be confidently wrong — verify facts before you trust them
  • Protect your privacy: never share passwords or personal details in a chat
  • Use AI to learn faster — not to skip the learning (that only cheats you)
  • Big decisions about people should always keep a human in charge
AI won't replace you. But a person who uses AI well just might.
— so become that person

Homework (the fun kind)

How to start — this week

  • Ask an AI to explain your hardest school topic like you're ten — then quiz yourself
  • Getting good at asking is a real skill — it's called prompting
  • Make something: a story, a poster, a tiny game — ship it, show a friend
  • Ask the AI to explain its answer — the “why” is where you learn
  • Rule of thumb: be curious, be sceptical, be kind
Try this prompt tonight
“Explain photosynthesis using a cricket match analogy, then give me 3 quiz questions to test myself.”
